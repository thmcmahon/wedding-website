Canberra

Getting there

  • Fly: Qantas and Virgin Australia have multiple flights to Canberra from capital cities every day. Demand for flights and accommodation is greater during periods when Parliament is sitting (Parliament will be sitting from 2-5 March 2015), so we recommend booking as far in advance as possible if you will be travelling at those times.
  • Drive: For the semi-adventurous and time-rich, the drive to Canberra takes about 14 hours from Adelaide and 8 hours from Melbourne. The Hay Plains are about the most boring drive you can take through Australia, but it’s worth doing (only) once.

Staying in Canberra

Have a browse of one of the many booking sites such as Wotif, Trivago etc. to get a good deal. Cabs are expensive and public transport can be hit-and-miss, so we’d suggest staying centrally – there are heaps of hotels/serviced apartments north of Lake Burley Griffin around Northbourne Ave in Civic, and the New Acton precinct is also a great spot for food and accommodation (seen recently in ABC’s The Code!).

South of the lake, if you’re looking for a piece of Canberra history, the Hotel Kurrajong has just been refurbished, and is close to the Kingston Foreshore - another good spot.

At the risk of offending residents, don’t bother with Canberra’s other satellite hubs, including Gungahlin, Belconnen, Woden or Tuggeranong – particularly if you won’t have a car to get around.

The wedding venue is close to Murrumbateman and Canberra’s cool climate wine district – there are places to stay out there, however you may be better off staying in Canberra and doing another day trip if you want to see more.

We can provide advice and recommendations if you’re not sure!

Things to do (and eat) in Canberra

Visitcanberra is a good place to start! Monday 9 March is the Canberra Day public holiday, so you you’ll be in the right place to celebrate the Capital.

Go see our national institutions, such as the War Memorial, Old and new Parliament Houses, National Gallery, National Library and the National Portrait Gallery.

Not that we’d argue with the New York Times anyway, but we can personally vouch for this assessment of an excellent way to spend a day or two.

Broadsheet have also done a pretty good hipster's guide to 72 hours in Canberra.

Canberra also has heaps of great restaurants including these for starters.

If you have a car, we also recommend the National Arboretum (great views over Canberra), Tidbinbilla Nature Reserve (beautiful walks, and you might even see an albino kangaroo), and around Murrumbateman for some wine tasting.

If you have some extra time to go further afield, Canberra is a couple of hours drive from the NSW South Coast. Make a day or two of it by visiting Bungendore and Braidwood on your way through.
